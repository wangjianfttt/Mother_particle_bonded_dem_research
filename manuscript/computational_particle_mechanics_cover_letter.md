# Cover letter

Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript entitled "Bonded-template DEM reveals strength- and topology-dependent fracture-event sequences in packed brittle ceramic pebbles" as a research article for *Computational Particle Mechanics*.

This manuscript presents a bonded-template DEM workflow for packed brittle-particle fracture. Each parent particle is represented by a 500-subparticle bonded template, inserted intact into a random load-bearing bed and compressed while parent-particle fracture events and native force-network descriptors are recorded. The repaired version includes an eleven-row material-response table and a six-case bond-strength matrix across two cracking geometries.

The main contribution is a particle-resolved event-sequence framework for brittle packed beds. Existing particle-replacement calculations are efficient for bed-scale breakage, bonded-particle models resolve internal fracture in individual objects and force-chain DEM describes load-path evolution. The present workflow links these levels by preserving many intact parent-particle bond graphs inside a random bed and exporting event tables with damaged-particle identity, bond-loss increments and native force-network state. The results show that early fracture in the finite packed-particle windows is controlled by local force-path topology together with bonded-particle strength.

The calculation is framed as computational particle-mechanics evidence for packed brittle ceramic pebbles. Li4SiO4 is used as a representative brittle ceramic application material with published single-particle crush data, while the method and state-variable analysis are relevant to packed brittle particles more broadly.

All figures are supported by source tables and regeneration scripts. A compact reproducibility package contains processed event tables, figure source data, scripts, representative DEM inputs, editable figures and a checksum manifest. Very large raw dump and restart files are retained in a separate NAS archive and can be supplied on reasonable request subject to repository and transfer limits.

The manuscript has not been published and is not under consideration elsewhere. The authors declare no competing interests.
