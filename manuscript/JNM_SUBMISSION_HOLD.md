# Journal of Nuclear Materials submission hold

Do not submit the current PDF or ZIP package.

The 2026-06-12 force-transmission audit found that the existing PB-006 beds have zero bottom reaction and no cross-mother-pebble contact at the reported first-fracture states. Their early damage is top-plate crushing of isolated upper pebbles, not collective fracture in a load-bearing bed.

Required before submission:

1. Complete and calibrate the surface-resolved single-pebble template.
2. Generate a corrected, mechanically connected random bed.
3. Verify zero deposition damage, nonzero bottom reaction, reaction balance and a pre-damage load-bearing contact network.
4. Replace the old PB-006 bed-scale figures, Results and Discussion.

See `docs/jnm_force_transmission_validation_audit_20260612.md`.


## 2026-06-12 PB-007 revision gate

A detailed withdrawal and replacement checklist is now recorded in `docs/jnm_revision_gate_after_pb006_audit_20260612.md`. In addition to the original hold conditions, the following items are explicitly not submission-ready:

- PB-006 Fig. 4-Fig. 8 and their associated Results/Discussion claims.
- PB-006 top-plate pressure curves interpreted as bed stress.
- PB-006 overlap-derived force-path proxy interpreted as force-chain evidence.
- PB-006 1000-pebble onset-quiet-burst and orientation-sensitivity claims.

PB-007 is the replacement route. It must pass zero-damage, native force-graph, six-wall force-output and gravity-baseline-corrected incremental force-balance gates before any new bed-scale fracture sequence is placed in the JNM main text.
