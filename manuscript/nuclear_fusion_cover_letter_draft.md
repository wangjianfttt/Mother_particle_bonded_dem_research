Dear Editors of Nuclear Fusion,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Mother-pebble-resolved fracture sequences in random Li4SiO4 breeder beds for fusion blankets by bonded-particle DEM" for consideration as a Paper in Nuclear Fusion.

Ceramic breeder pebble beds are central to tritium breeding and heat removal in helium-cooled blanket concepts, but pebble crushing remains difficult to connect across scales. Single-pebble crush tests provide contact-strength information, and bed-compression tests provide macroscopic responses, yet neither directly resolves the order and location of fracture events inside individual pebbles in an opaque bed. This manuscript addresses that gap with a bonded-template DEM workflow in which each nominal 1 mm Li4SiO4 mother pebble is represented by 500 bonded subparticles, inserted intact into random packings, and compressed while tracking mother-pebble-resolved internal bond failure.

The main technical advance is a locked-template procedure that separates random bed formation from pebble fracture. Proxy spheres are first gravity-settled; their centres are then replaced by bonded templates that are created far apart to form only internal bonds and translated into the packing as intact groups. This procedure preserves zero internal bond loss before compression in both 500- and 1000-pebble beds. The resulting local bond histories reveal reproducible top-layer first breakage in three independent 500-pebble beds, while post-onset growth depends strongly on upper-bed packing. Restartable 1000-pebble simulations to 0.15 mm further show a robust top-layer trigger but orientation- and packing-sensitive propagation, with broken-bond totals ranging from 95 to 316 across the completed 1000-pebble event-sequence cases.

We believe the work fits Nuclear Fusion because it provides a reproducible computational route for linking single-pebble crushing, random breeder-bed packing structure and bed-scale fracture-event evolution, which are all relevant to the mechanical reliability of fusion blanket breeder beds. The manuscript is deliberately conservative in scope: the selected bonded template is treated as a current calibration candidate rather than a final Li4SiO4 material law, and the overlap-derived force-path analysis is presented as a diagnostic rather than direct native force-chain evidence.

All authors have approved the manuscript submission. The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and does not contain previously published material except where properly cited. Processed figure data, event tables, representative simulation inputs and post-processing scripts have been assembled in a reduced reproducibility package with a checksum manifest; the persistent repository DOI or stable URL should be inserted before final upload.
