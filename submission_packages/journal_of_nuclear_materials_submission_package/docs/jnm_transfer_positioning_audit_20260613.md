# JNM transfer-positioning audit

Purpose: keep the Journal of Nuclear Materials submission positioned as a nuclear-materials degradation paper after the Nuclear Fusion scope-fit rejection.

## Transfer logic

The Nuclear Fusion decision stated that the subject was too specialised for the Nuclear Fusion audience and recommended a more specialised nuclear materials journal. This is treated as a scope-fit signal, not as evidence that the calculation, figures or manuscript argument are invalid. The transfer response is therefore not to broaden the paper into a reactor-design claim, but to sharpen the materials question:

> How can early fracture of Li4SiO4 breeder pebbles inside an opaque bed be made visible as a time-ordered, mother-pebble-resolved damage sequence, and how does that damage relate to the evolving load-bearing contact skeleton of a functional nuclear ceramic material?

## JNM-facing argument

The manuscript is positioned for Journal of Nuclear Materials because it studies Li4SiO4 as a ceramic breeder material in a solid breeder blanket. The central object is not a generic granular bed; it is a mechanically loaded nuclear ceramic inventory whose local cracking can alter contact stiffness, thermal-contact constrictions and helium purge-path tortuosity.

The paper's novelty is framed around three linked materials-modelling advances:

1. Intact insertion of 500-subparticle bonded mother-pebble templates into a random bed without seating damage.
2. Acceptance gates that reject invalid force-transmission states before internal bond loss is interpreted.
3. Mother-pebble-resolved fracture-event extraction linked to native force-network topology and macroscopic response.

This makes the numerical workflow a diagnostic instrument for ceramic breeder-material degradation rather than an end in itself.

## Claims that must remain central

- Li4SiO4 pebbles are functional ceramic breeder materials, not merely model spheres.
- Localized internal bond loss is interpreted as early ceramic microcracking, not as a converged bed-failure probability.
- Native force-network descriptors are used as a mesoscale activation and shielding field for damage initiation.
- The independent intact bed and strength-window audits support local force-path sensitivity, not universal toughness.
- Thermal-contact and purge-flow language is kept as service relevance and future coupling motivation, not as a solved coupled transport result.

## Phrases to avoid in submission-facing text

- "blanket design margin predicted"
- "converged failure probability"
- "final Li4SiO4 material law"
- "thermal-hydraulic model"
- "reactor design calculation"
- "generic DEM study"

These phrases may appear only as explicit negative boundaries.

## Author/editorial use

Use this audit to keep the cover letter, highlights, abstract and response-to-editor wording aligned. If an editor asks why the manuscript was transferred from Nuclear Fusion, the concise answer is:

> Nuclear Fusion identified the topic as too specialised for its broad fusion audience. The manuscript has therefore been repositioned for Journal of Nuclear Materials as a nuclear ceramic breeder-material degradation study, with conservative claims tied to single-pebble calibration, accepted load-path gates, fracture-event sequence data and native force-network evidence.
