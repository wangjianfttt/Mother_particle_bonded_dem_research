## Short summary for submission system

This manuscript presents a bonded-template discrete-element workflow for resolving mother-pebble fracture-event sequences in random Li4SiO4 ceramic breeder beds. Each nominal 1 mm mother pebble is represented by a 500-subparticle bonded template and inserted intact into gravity-settled random beds so that internal bond failure is absent during deposition and activated only during compression. Three 500-pebble beds and three 1000-pebble event-sequence cases show a robust top-layer fracture trigger but packing- and orientation-sensitive post-onset propagation.

## Suggested highlights

- A locked bonded-template DEM workflow inserts 500-subparticle Li4SiO4 mother pebbles into random beds with zero precompression bond loss.
- Three 500-pebble random beds show reproducible first breakage in the highest mother pebble but seed-dependent post-onset cascade growth.
- Restartable 1000-pebble calculations resolve top-layer onset and orientation/packing-sensitive propagation to 0.15 mm compression.
- The study provides event-sequence evidence and a reproducible workflow, while treating the selected weak-plane template as a calibration candidate rather than a final Li4SiO4 material law.
